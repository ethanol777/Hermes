## Key Observations

1. **DDR4 vs DDR5 divergence**: DDR4 consumer module prices are actually falling slightly (-3% to -6% weekly) in April 2026, while DDR5 is rising (+10% monthly on server side)
2. **DDR4 EOL effect**: DDR4 chip prices have risen 683% from Jan 2025 trough but consumer module prices suggest potential demand softening
3. **DDR5 server demand**: RDIMM prices rising 10%+ monthly driven by AI server buildout
4. **NAND stable**: Flash wafer prices unchanged on May 8, 2026 — the NAND market seems more balanced than DRAM
5. **Chinese retail (RMB)**: DDR4 16GB ≈ ¥550-600, DDR5 32GB ≈ ¥900-1,100 at current exchange rates
